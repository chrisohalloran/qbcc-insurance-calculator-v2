About QBCC Home Warranty Insurance

What is QBCC Home Warranty Insurance?

QBCC Home Warranty Insurance is mandatory insurance for residential construction work in Queensland. It is part of the Queensland Home Warranty Scheme, administered by the Queensland Building and Construction Commission (QBCC).

The insurance protects homeowners if their licensed contractor dies, disappears, becomes insolvent, or has their licence suspended or cancelled — either before the project is finished or after completion where defective work is left unresolved. It is legally separate from the contractor's own public liability insurance and covers the homeowner directly.

The scheme applies to new home construction, renovations, extensions, additions, and other residential building work above the minimum threshold. Commercial construction and owner-builder projects are not covered. See When is QBCC insurance required? for the full list of inclusions and exclusions.

What does QBCC insurance cover?

QBCC insurance covers residential construction work up to $3.3 million in insurable value. It protects homeowners against financial loss when a licensed contractor:

  • dies, disappears, or becomes insolvent before completing the work
  • has their licence suspended or cancelled
  • fails to complete the contracted work
  • leaves defective or incomplete work they refuse or are unable to fix

The cover also extends to subsidence (soil movement causing structural damage) for covered work.

The scheme does not cover general disputes over cost or delays while the contractor is still licensed and trading, commercial construction, work below the $3,300 threshold, or work carried out by an owner-builder.

The maximum amount QBCC can pay out on a claim depends on the insurable value of the contract. Contact QBCC directly for specific coverage limits on your project.

Who pays for QBCC insurance?

The licensed contractor (your builder) is legally required to take out and pay the QBCC insurance premium. The cost is almost always passed through to the homeowner as part of the contract price — but the legal obligation to obtain cover sits with the contractor, not the homeowner.

The contractor must obtain a Notice of Cover from QBCC before accepting any deposit or starting any work. If your contractor hasn't provided a Notice of Cover number before asking for a deposit, that is a serious compliance issue — contact QBCC on 139 333.

Before and During Your Project

When is QBCC insurance required?

QBCC Home Warranty Insurance is required for any residential construction work in Queensland where the contract price exceeds $3,300 (including both labour and materials). This threshold applies to:

  • new home construction
  • renovations, extensions, and additions
  • swimming pools and garages attached to a residence
  • other residential building work above the threshold

Insurance must be taken out before any work begins and before any deposit is accepted. The contractor cannot start the job or ask for money until the Notice of Cover is in place.

Some work is exempt — including commercial construction, work by an owner-builder with a valid permit, and certain specialist trades where the contract price is below the threshold. For a full breakdown of what does and doesn't require insurance, see our guide: When is QBCC Home Warranty Insurance required?

What's the difference between new construction and renovation?

New construction is building a new residential dwelling from the ground up — a house, duplex, townhouse, or similar structure on land where no existing home is being modified.

Renovation covers alterations, additions, and repairs to an existing residential structure. This includes extensions, internal renovations, new rooms, decks, and substantial repairs.

QBCC charges different premium rates for each project type — renovation premiums are consistently higher than new construction at the same contract value. The difference is significant:

Using This Calculator

How accurate is this calculator?

The calculator uses the official QBCC premium table effective 1 July 2020, which remains the current rate schedule as of July 2026. Premiums are calculated from the insurable value (contract price) you enter, separately for new construction and renovation projects.

The estimate also includes the QLeave Levy — the 0.575% portable long service leave levy that applies to most Queensland residential construction contracts. This is calculated on the insurable value (which includes the QBCC premium itself, per QBCC's own calculation method).

The combined estimate is a reliable guide for quoting and budgeting. The final premium charged by QBCC may differ slightly depending on your specific contract details. Making a Claim

How do I make a claim on QBCC insurance?

Claims are lodged directly with QBCC — not through this calculator or your builder. Contact QBCC on 139 333 or visit qbcc.qld.gov.au to start the process. You will need your Notice of Cover number, the contract details, and documentation of the issue.

Time limits apply and are strictly enforced. Key deadlines from QBCC's Queensland Home Warranty Scheme rules:

Claim typeTime limit
Non-completion (contract terminated)Contract must end within 2 years of work starting; lodge claim within 3 months of contract ending
Structural defectsBecome aware within 6 years 6 months of cover commencement; lodge claim within 3 months of becoming aware
Non-structural defectsBecome aware within 6 months of substantial completion; lodge claim within 7 months of substantial completion

Source: QBCC — Time limits for cover and claims

If your situation is urgent or you are unsure whether a claim applies, call QBCC on 139 333 before the deadline passes. Do not wait for the contractor to resolve the issue — QBCC will assess independently.
