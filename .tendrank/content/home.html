Renovation (alteration) premiums are higher than new-construction premiums at the same value, so match the project type to the work you are actually pricing. Get that wrong and your estimate can be out by hundreds of dollars.

What the calculator estimates

Two separate government costs apply to most residential building work in Queensland. The calculator handles both:

  • QBCC home warranty insurance premium — required on residential construction work valued at $3,300 or more. Your contractor takes out the cover and pays the premium before work begins; the cost is usually passed on in the contract price.
  • QLeave levy (0.575%) — payable on residential construction work valued at $150,000 or more (excluding GST), on top of the insurance premium.

The two amounts interact. The QLeave levy forms part of the insurable value used to work out the QBCC premium, so the numbers are not simply added side by side. The calculator applies the correct order and shows a single combined figure.

Why estimate before you commit

The premium and levy are real, mandatory costs that sit on top of your build price. Builders who leave them out of a quote absorb the difference. Homeowners who do not ask about them can be surprised at contract signing. A quick estimate up front puts the number in the price from the start, not in a dispute later.

Because the QBCC premium jumps between rate bands as the insurable value rises, a small change in project value can move the premium more than you expect. Run the figure before you finalise the contract value to avoid a shortfall.
